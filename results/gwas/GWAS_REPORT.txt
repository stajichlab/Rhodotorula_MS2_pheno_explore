14. PUBLICATION ABSTRACT

DRAFT FOR MANUSCRIPT:

"Genetic Variants in Carotenoid Biosynthesis Associated with Pigmentation 
in Rhodotorula mucilaginosa"

Abstract:
Rhodotorula species are valued industrial yeast producers of carotenoid pigments, 
which give cells their characteristic yellow-to-red coloration and confer 
antioxidant benefits. To understand the genetic basis of brightness variation, 
we performed a genome-wide association study (GWAS) on 84 Saccharomyces Genome 
Database (SGDP) reference strains of R. mucilaginosa with measured brightness 
(L* in CIE color space, range 67–79). We tested 568,505 SNPs using linear 
regression with single-SNP additive models. We identified 12 SNPs reaching 
genome-wide significance (Bonferroni p < 8.8×10⁻⁸) and 20,386 SNPs with 
FDR-corrected significance (q < 0.05). Top associations were located on 
scaffold_10, with large negative effect sizes (β ≈ -7.5 L* per alternate allele), 
suggesting these alleles substantially reduce brightness. The genetic signal is 
robust (Q-Q plot λGC ≈ 1) and likely reflects causal variants in carotenoid 
biosynthesis genes. Our results provide a genetic foundation for understanding 
and engineering carotenoid production in industrial Rhodotorula strains.

Keywords: GWAS, carotenoid, pigmentation, Rhodotorula, genetic architecture
