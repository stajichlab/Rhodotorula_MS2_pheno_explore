3. ANALYTICAL APPROACH
================================================================================

PLEIOTROPY TESTING METHODOLOGY:

For each Bonferroni-significant SNP:
  1. Extract genotypes (0, 1, 2 copies of alternate allele)
  2. Test association with brightness phenotype: brightness ~ SNP genotype
  3. Test association with metabolite: metabolite ~ SNP genotype
  4. Compare effect directions and p-values
  5. Classify as: PLEIOTROPIC (both traits significant) or SNP-SPECIFIC

DEFINITION OF PLEIOTROPY:
  ✓ SNP affects brightness: p < 0.05
  ✓ SNP affects metabolite: p < 0.05
  ✓ Effect directions agree (same sign of beta)
  = PLEIOTROPIC EFFECT: SNP has multiple functional roles

INTERPRETATION:
  • Pleiotropic SNPs → direct pathway involvement
  • SNP-specific effects → indirect or tissue-specific effects
  • No effect on metabolite → regulatory role only
